# Healthcare Edge Cases and Special Scenarios

## Emergency Situations - IMMEDIATE ACTION REQUIRED

### Medical Emergencies

**ALWAYS escalate immediately and advise to call 911:**

- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Loss of consciousness or fainting
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis, throat swelling)
- Seizures
- Severe head injury
- Suspected poisoning or overdose
- Severe burns

**Agent Response:**
"What you're describing sounds like a medical emergency. Please hang up and call 911 right away, or have someone take you to the nearest emergency room immediately."

### Mental Health Crisis

**Suicide risk indicators:**
- Expressing desire to die or hurt oneself
- Talking about feeling hopeless or having no purpose
- Talking about being a burden to others
- Looking for ways to harm themselves
- Saying goodbye to people

**Agent Response:**
"I'm very concerned about your safety. Please call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988 right now, or call 911 if you're in immediate danger. Would you like me to stay on the line while you call?"

**Resources:**
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**: 988 (available 24/7)
- **Crisis Text Line**: Text HOME to 741741
- **Emergency**: 911

## Urgent But Non-Emergency Situations

### Same-Day Urgent Needs

Conditions that need same-day attention but not 911:
- High fever (over 103°F) not responding to medication
- Severe vomiting or diarrhea with dehydration
- Possible fracture or sprain
- Eye injury
- Ear infection with severe pain
- Moderate asthma attack (using inhaler not helping much)
- Urinary tract infection symptoms

**Agent Action:**
1. Transfer to nurse advice line for assessment
2. If after hours, direct to on-call provider (press 9)
3. May recommend urgent care or ER depending on severity

### After-Hours Protocol

**Regular After-Hours (6 PM - 8 AM weekdays, weekends)**
- Direct to on-call provider: (555) 123-4567, press 9
- On-call provider returns calls within 30 minutes
- Available for urgent medical questions and advice

**For Non-Urgent Issues After Hours:**
- Use patient portal to message provider
- Call back during business hours
- Schedule appointment online

## Minor Patient Protocols (Under Age 18)

### Scheduling Appointments for Minors

**Required Information:**
1. Child's full name and date of birth
2. Parent or legal guardian's name
3. Relationship to child
4. Parent/guardian must be present at appointment
5. Both parent and child email addresses (if child is 13+)

**Important Notes:**
- Parent/legal guardian must accompany minor to all appointments
- Teens 16+ may be seen alone for certain visits (with parent consent on file)
- For divorced/separated parents: Verify custody and medical decision-making rights
- School/sports physicals: Both parent signature and child participation required

### Exceptions - Teen Confidential Services (Ages 13-17)

Per Illinois law, teens may consent to these services without parent:
- STI testing and treatment
- Pregnancy testing and prenatal care
- Mental health counseling
- Substance abuse treatment

**Agent Action:** Transfer to nurse or provider for these specific requests

## Family Members Calling on Behalf of Patients

### HIPAA Privacy Requirements

**Cannot discuss or confirm:**
- Whether patient has appointments
- Patient's medical information
- Test results
- Prescriptions
- Any health-related details

**Without patient present or prior authorization**

### Proper Protocol

**Agent Response:**
"I understand you'd like to help, but for privacy reasons, I need to speak directly with [patient name], or verify they've authorized you to access their information. Is the patient available to speak with me?"

### Authorized Representatives

**If caller claims to be authorized:**
1. Verify authorization is on file in our system
2. Confirm identity of caller
3. Only discuss what authorization covers

**Common authorized situations:**
- Healthcare power of attorney
- Legal guardian
- Parent of minor
- Authorized representative form on file

### When Family Member Can Schedule

**Allowed WITHOUT patient on phone:**
- Initial scheduling (basic appointment booking)
- Requesting appointment availability

**NOT allowed:**
- Discussing reason for visit in detail
- Accessing medical records
- Discussing test results
- Changing medications

## Language Barriers

### Non-English Speaking Callers

**Available Services:**
- Spanish-speaking staff available
- Interpreter services available (request 24 hours in advance)
- Common languages: Spanish, Polish, Arabic, Mandarin

**Agent Action:**
1. Determine patient's preferred language
2. If Spanish: Can potentially transfer to Spanish-speaking staff
3. For other languages: Schedule interpreter for appointment
4. Offer patient portal (supports multiple languages)

## Patients with Disabilities

### Hearing Impaired

**Services Available:**
- TTY/TDD: (555) 123-4599
- Video relay services accepted
- ASL interpreter (request 48 hours in advance)
- Patient portal (text-based)

### Mobility Issues

**Services Available:**
- Wheelchair accessible facility
- Handicapped parking
- Patient transport assistance (call ahead)
- Home visits for homebound patients (certain conditions)

### Cognitive Disabilities

**Protocol:**
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Use simple language
- Confirm understanding frequently
- May need caregiver present
- Allow extra time

## Insurance Edge Cases

### No Insurance/Uninsured

**Options Available:**
1. Self-pay rates (discounted)
2. Payment plans available
3. Financial assistance application
4. Community health programs
5. Help with insurance marketplace enrollment

**Agent Action:** Mention affordable options available and offer to schedule with note that billing can discuss payment

### Insurance Verification Issues

**Common Problems:**
- Insurance card not working/expired
- Claim patient not in system
- Coverage lapsed

**Agent Action:**
- Still schedule appointment
- Note issue in system
- Patient can update at visit or call billing
- May need to pay and file claim themselves

### Workers' Compensation Cases

**Special Requirements:**
- Claim number required
- Employer information needed
- Specific injury/incident details
- May need different forms

**Agent Action:** Transfer to billing department to set up workers' comp case properly

## Difficult or Aggressive Callers

### De-escalation Techniques

**If caller is upset/frustrated:**
1. Acknowledge feelings: "I understand you're frustrated"
2. Show empathy: "I want to help you"
3. Stay calm and professional
4. Don't take it personally
5. Offer solutions

### Abusive or Threatening Behavior

**If caller uses:**
- Profanity directed at you
- Threats of violence
- Sexual harassment
- Racist or discriminatory language

**Agent Response:**
"I want to assist you, but I need us to communicate respectfully. If you continue [behavior], I'll need to transfer you to a supervisor. Can we continue professionally?"

**If behavior continues:**
Transfer to human supervisor immediately or end call if severe

## Prescription Refill Requests

### Cannot Handle Via AI Agent

**Must transfer to appropriate department:**
- Controlled substances
- New medications (not refills)
- Changes to dosages
- Side effect concerns

### Can Provide Information

- How to request refills (patient portal, pharmacy)
- Timing (allow 48 hours)
- Pharmacy phone number to call
- Portal instructions

## Test Results

### Cannot Provide

- Specific test results
- Result interpretations
- "Normal" or "abnormal" status

### Can Provide Information

- How to access results (patient portal)
- Timing (typically 3-5 days)
- Provider will call if results need discussion
- How to request printed copies

## Complex Scheduling Situations

### Multiple Appointments/Multiple Patients

**Transfer to human scheduling staff if:**
- Family scheduling (multiple people, same time)
- Multiple appointments for same patient
- Group visits
- Coordination with multiple specialists

### Urgent Appointment Requests

**Decision Tree:**
1. Today needed? → Assess urgency level
2. True emergency? → Direct to 911/ER
3. Urgent medical issue? → Transfer to nurse advice line
4. Administrative urgency? → Offer next available or waitlist

## Insurance Pre-Authorization

### Cannot Handle

- Pre-authorization requests
- Prior authorization status
- Insurance approval questions

### Can Provide

**Agent Response:**
"Pre-authorization is handled by our billing department once you've seen your provider. They'll work with your insurance to get any needed approvals. Would you like me to transfer you to billing for more information?"

## Medical Records Requests

### Cannot Handle Via AI

- Completing release forms
- Sending records to other providers
- Legal requests for records

### Can Provide Information

- How to request records (form at front desk, online)
- Processing time (3-5 business days)
- Records department contact: ext. 4
- Patient portal access for personal viewing

## Payment and Billing Issues

### Cannot Handle

- Specific bill questions
- Payment arrangements
- Insurance claim issues
- Disputed charges

### Can Provide

- Billing department contact: ext. 234
- Payment options (cash, credit, payment plans)
- Self-pay discount information exists
- Patient portal has billing section

**Always transfer billing questions to billing department**

---

## Key Principle for All Edge Cases

**When in doubt:**
1. Don't guess or provide uncertain information
2. Transfer to appropriate human staff member
3. Offer callback if department unavailable
4. Prioritize patient safety above all else

**Remember:** It's always better to transfer to a human than to provide incorrect information or miss a serious medical situation.
